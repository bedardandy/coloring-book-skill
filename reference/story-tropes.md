# Story tropes — a menu of proven structures for ages 3-6

How to use: when the user hasn't specified a story, offer 2-3 tropes matched to the
child's age and interests (name + one-sentence pitch each), then instantiate the chosen
one onto the 10-beat arc in story-mode.md. Slots: [Kid], [Sib] (sibling/cousin/friend),
[Pet], [Interest] (dinosaurs, mermaids...), [Place] (backyard, grandma's house).
All captions still follow story-mode.md rules (present tense, name repetition, refrain,
no rhyme, cozy obstacles only).

## Engagement mechanics (mix 1-2 into any trope)
- **Refrain/chant** — a repeated line every 1-2 pages kids say along ("Glow, garden, glow!")
- **Cumulative repetition** — each page adds one element and re-lists the chain
  (the Great-Big-Turnip pattern); gold for 3-4, cap the chain at ~4 items
- **Count-up / countdown** — number woven into each page (find 1, 2, 3...); pairs
  naturally with find-the-X activity pages
- **Page-turn question** — end a page on "Who is behind the gate?" so the turn answers it
- **Sound words** — Vroom! Splash! Boing! One per page max, big friendly type
- **Reader participation** — "Pat Pip's head. Now turn the page!" (Press-Here style);
  in a coloring book this can be literal: "color the lantern to light it!"

## Age fit principles
- **3-4**: repetition and naming over plot; a resolution on every page; no real suspense;
  cumulative/counting structures; the child does one clear action per page.
- **5-6**: light suspense fine (a secret, a search, "will it work?"); three-tries
  patterns; humor via role reversal; still zero villains — obstacles stay hidden/shy/
  sleepy/wobbly, never threatening. Being "lost" is always hide-and-seek toned.
- **Universal**: the child acts and solves; adults stay background; a food/party/
  celebration beat near the end; final page = cozy return (home, bed, hug).

## Classic tropes (each maps cleanly onto the 10-beat arc)

1. **The Lost Treasure** — [Kid]'s beloved thing goes missing; search place by place;
   [Pet] finds it. Beats 5-7 = three search locations. Hook: refrain + page-turn
   questions. Ages 3-6. (The bundled example book is this trope.)
2. **Try, Try Again** — [Kid] learns [skill: bike, cartwheel, whistling]; three funny
   failed attempts, each teaching one thing; success using all three lessons. Beats
   5-7 = the three attempts. Hook: sound words per attempt. Ages 4-6; growth-mindset.
   Art: bicycle/swing_set helpers shine here.
3. **The Helper** — a small creature needs help (bird's nest fell, kitten stuck, snail
   lost); [Kid]+[Sib] gather three things/friends to fix it; thank-you celebration.
   Hook: cumulative chain of helpers. Ages 3-6; kindness payoff.
4. **The Big First Day** — first day of [school/swim class/sleepover]; nervous morning,
   three small brave moments, proud ending. Beats 5-7 = the brave moments. Hook:
   a comfort object cameo (their real lovey). Ages 4-6; emotional arc, keep wins tiny.
5. **The Surprise Party** — kids secretly prepare a party for [Pet]/[Grandparent]; each
   kid has one job; near-miss ("hide the cake!"); the reveal. Hook: reader kept in on
   the secret ("Shhh! Don't tell!"). Ages 4-6.
6. **The Counting Walk** — a walk through [Place] finding 1, 2, 3... of things tied to
   [Interest]; ends counting everyone at home. Hook: count-up + find-the-X on every
   page. Ages 3-4 especially; nearly plotless by design.
7. **Build It Together** — [Kid]+[Sib] build a [fort/rocket/lemonade stand]; each page
   adds one part; finale = using it. Beats 5-7 = three parts, each with a wobble fixed.
   Hook: cumulative re-list of parts. Ages 3-6. Art: furniture/vehicle helpers.
8. **The Magic Door** — an ordinary thing ([garden gate/wardrobe/big cardboard box])
   opens into [Interest]-land; three wonders explored; home in time for dinner ("was
   it real? [Pet]'s collar has stardust!"). Ages 4-6. Keep the land soft and sunny.
9. **The Bedtime Round** — goodnight-moon pattern: say goodnight to everything in the
   room/house, one page each, family last. Hook: naming + one whispered refrain.
   Ages 3-4; the classic wind-down book; pairs with a nightlight coloring page.
10. **The Weather Twist** — rain/snow/wind threatens the plan; kids invent a better
    indoor/adapted version. Hook: sound words (Drip! Whoosh!). Ages 3-6; teaches
    flexible thinking; obstacle is weather, never danger.

## Creative tropes (fresher angles that still test well)

11. **The Pet's Secret Day** — what does [Pet] do when everyone leaves? "Helps" with
    chores, badly and adorably; kids come home to the funny evidence and [Pet] pretends
    to sleep. Hook: reader sees what the kids don't. Ages 4-6; comedy of the gap.
12. **The Very Shy Monster** — something rustles in [Place]... it's a small round
    creature who just wants to play; kids teach it their favorite game. Gentle
    subversion: "scary" resolves to shy by page 5, never later. Round shapes, no
    teeth/claws. Ages 4-6; empathy payoff.
13. **Backwards Day** — opens at the celebration ("The End! ...wait, how did we get
    here?") then hops back through "because..." pages to the start, then a fast
    re-run forward. Ages 5-6 only; the reversal is the joke; keep each "because" one
    sentence.
14. **The Tiny Explorers** — kids shrink to bug size in their own [backyard/bedroom];
    a puddle is an ocean, [Pet] is a mountain; ride a paper boat home and pop back to
    size at the dinner bell. Ages 4-6. Art: familiar helpers drawn huge = free wonder.
15. **Swap Day** — [Kid] runs the [store/post office/vet clinic] for a day; family
    and [Pet] visit as customers with silly requests; closing-time tally. Hook:
    repeated transaction pattern with escalating silliness. Ages 4-6.
16. **Grandparent's Mystery Box** — [Grandparent] leaves a box with three simple
    clues leading through family places to a small treasure (photo, cookie recipe,
    seeds to plant together). Ages 4-6; intergenerational warmth; clues are pictures,
    not riddles.
17. **The Reader Helps** — fourth-wall book: the kids ask the READER to help on every
    page — color the sun to warm them, trace the path to show the way, "shake the book
    gently" to knock the apple down. Ages 3-5; maximum engagement, minimum plot;
    every request must be literally colorable/traceable on the page.
18. **The Quiet Wonder Walk** — a nearly plotless dusk/dawn walk: fireflies, the moon
    following, one deer moment, warm windows of home at the end. Ages 3-6; the
    atmospheric bedtime option when the family wants calm over adventure.

## Instantiation checklist
- Confirm the trope + interests mapping with the user before building pages.
- Keep the trope's page-type variety obligations (cover, name-trace, one activity page).
- Cumulative/counting tropes: the activity page IS the mechanic (count/find on-page).
- Never stack two high-energy mechanics; one refrain OR one counting pattern per book.
